History

History of EB Garamond

EB Garamond is intended to be an excellent, classical, Garamond. It is a community project to create a revival of Claude Garamond's famous humanist typefaces from the mid-16th century. Design reproduces the original.
       The source from the letterforms is a scan of a specimen known as the "Berner specimen," which was composed in 1592 by Conrad Berner.
       Why another Garamond? This typeface was a key moment in the history of typography, and European type designers have been working on this ever since.

Original author

Claude Garamond was know as a French type designer, publisher and punch-cutter based in Paris. He worked as an engrave in a tradition now called old-style serif design. It produced letters with a relatively organic structure resembling handwriting with a pen. He was one of the leading type designers of all time.
      Garamond was one of the first independent punchcutters, specializing in type design and punch-cutting as a service to others.

The revival

By Georg Mayr-Duffner. George's path to creating fonts started with learning LaTeX, which was created to improve computer typography, and the principles of good typography.
         When it came to writing his master thesis, he was looking for a font to set the thesis in. He started the EB Garamond project after unsuccessfully searching for an open source Garamond font that provided advanced typographic features as well as Greek and phonetic characters.
